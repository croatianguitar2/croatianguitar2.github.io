About

The Croatian Guitar Duo, formed by Edin Karamazov and Melita Ivković, brings together two artistic voices in a collaboration shaped by close musical dialogue, technical precision, and a shared interest in exploring the guitar repertoire beyond conventional formats. Their performances are characterised by a thoughtful balance between historical awareness and contemporary interpretation.

Edin Karamazov is internationally recognised as one of the leading guitarists and lutenists of his generation. Renowned for his interpretations spanning Renaissance and Baroque music to the modern repertoire, he has appeared at many of the world’s major concert venues, including the Concertgebouw Amsterdam, Wigmore Hall London, and the Berlin Philharmonie. His extensive discography includes collaborations with artists such as Sting and Andreas Scholl, reflecting an artistic profile that combines scholarly depth with creative openness.

Melita Ivković is a performing artist and educator active on the international concert scene for more than three decades, with over 500 performances in 32 countries. She is co-founder of Dina e Mel, an ethnology-based world-music project focused on the research and reinterpretation of traditional vocal material, and has also been engaged in teaching, cultural organisation, and festival work.

At the centre of the duo’s repertoire are original arrangements for two guitars by Edin Karamazov, bringing together works by composers from different historical periods, including Joseph Haydn, Sergei Prokofiev, and Johann Sebastian Bach. Through these arrangements, the duo explores a wide stylistic range—from Classical formal clarity to modernist expression and Baroque contrapuntal writing—while extending the expressive scope of the guitar duo.

The Croatian Guitar Duo’s performances emphasise musical dialogue and structural clarity, offering programmes that invite attentive listening and reveal new perspectives on familiar works.
