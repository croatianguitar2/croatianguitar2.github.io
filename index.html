About

The Croatian Guitar Duo, formed by two distinguished guitarists—Edin Karamazov and Melita Ivković—presents a programme that unites refined stylistic insight with technical brilliance, offering audiences a concert experience in which the expressive potential of the guitar reaches its highest artistic levels.

The repertoire includes works by Joseph Haydn, Sergei Prokofiev, and Johann Sebastian Bach, arranged for two guitars by Edin Karamazov. Through carefully conceived interpretations, this musical dialogue between two masterful performers explores the expressive and structural limits of the guitar duo format, ranging from Classical elegance and the subtlety of Baroque counterpoint to the expressive modernity of the twentieth century.

Melita Ivković is a performing artist and educator active on the international concert scene for more than three decades, with over 500 performances in 32 countries. She is co-founder of Dina e Mel, an ethnology-based world-music project focused on the research and reinterpretation of traditional vocal material, and has also been engaged in teaching, cultural organisation, and festival work.

Edin Karamazov is among the most original and highly regarded guitarists and lutenists of today, renowned for his interpretative precision and distinctive approach to the repertoire. His performances are marked by profound analysis of the musical text, a spirit of inquiry, and exceptional technical command. Karamazov has appeared in the world’s most prestigious concert halls and collaborated with leading artists such as Sting, Andreas Scholl, and Dorothee Oberlinger. His extensive discography bears witness to his artistic curiosity and his continual pursuit of new interpretative horizons.

Melita Ivković is a musician whose artistic career encompasses performances in thirty-two countries across four continents, alongside significant pedagogical work and leadership of international music festivals. Her interpretations combine virtuosity with a deep understanding of stylistic nuance, while her work as a member of the Zagreb Guitar Quartet and the ethnomusicological project Dina e Mel further affirms her commitment to expanding the boundaries of the guitar repertoire.
